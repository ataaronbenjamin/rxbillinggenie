Understanding Adjudication and Rejection Codes in Pharmacy Billing

Shelley Morgan
October 9, 2024
4 min read

Pharmacy billing is an essential part of healthcare delivery, ensuring patients receive their medications without unnecessary financial burden. Claims don't always move through smoothly — and this is where adjudication intervention codes and rejection codes come in. These codes are fundamental tools that help streamline — or flag issues in — the billing process.

Adjudication Intervention Codes are used when there is a discrepancy or issue in the initial insurance claim processing. These codes offer a way to intervene in real time, making required modifications that may allow the claim to proceed without delay. They often involve direct correspondence between the pharmacy and the insurance company to ensure all details are correct. For example, during adjudication with organizations like Green Shield Canada, these codes play a significant role in clearing up mistakes and moving the claim forward.

On the other hand, adjudication rejection codes explain why an insurance company has rejected a claim. Rejection can result from inaccurate patient information, expired coverage, or exceeding the allowed dosage — among many other variables. Understanding these rejection codes is essential for pharmacies to resubmit claims correctly. Pharmacists should treat rejections from companies like ESI or Assure as actionable information, not just setbacks.

Why Should Pharmacies Care?

Managing both adjudication and rejection codes ensures that pharmacies are paid on time, minimizes delays in claim processing, and promotes overall billing efficiency. Mishandling rejection situations can result in wasted revenue and frustrated patients.

Pharmacists who use intervention codes correctly and respond promptly to rejection codes can maintain a clean billing cycle, reduce error rates, and strengthen their working relationships with insurance adjudicators.
